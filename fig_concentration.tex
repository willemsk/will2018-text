\caption[\textbf{Ion concentration distribution inside ClyA.}]
{
\textbf{Ion concentration distribution inside ClyA.}
Relative ion concentrations averaged over the entire pore in function of (a) bias voltage and (b) bulk salt 
concentration for both \ce{Na+} (full blue lines) and \ce{Cl-} (dotted red lines). Note that in general there 
is a considerable enhancement for \ce{Na+} and a significant depletion for \ce{Cl-}, even at physiological 
concentrations (\SI{\approx150}{\mM}). Both effects diminish with increasing concentration and bulk-like 
conditions are observed for both ions at \SI{\approx0.5}{\Molar}.
(c) Contour plots of the \ce{Na+} (top) and \ce{Cl-} (bottom) concentrations inside the pore relative to the 
bulk value of \SI{150}{\mM} at bias voltages \SI{-100}{\mV} (left) and \SI{+100}{\mV} (right). These plots 
indicate that under most conditions the ion concentrations in the lumen of the pore are bulk like, with the 
constrictions showing the most drastic changes. Under negative bias voltages however, the lumen of the pore 
becomes fully depleted from \ce{Cl-}.
(d) Radially averaged relative ion concentration profiles along the z-axis of pore for \ce{Na+} (top) and 
\ce{Cl-} (bottom) at \SI{-100}{\mV} (red) and \SI{+100}{\mV} (blue).
